I Am A Reason --??

Last Updated on March 30, 2020    ·    4 min read    ·    Coca-Cola

As part of their I am A Reason campaign the coca cola company has made this billboard that says "I got her consent after 32 attempts", in hopes of encouraging persistence & hard-work. But 32 times of what? Texts? Calls? Asking her out? Following her? What did this "persistence" involve?
When does it become Harassment or stalking? In my book this isn't the kind of persistence we should be celebrating. I understand that each case and context has its twists and we should have room for people's choices. We've a billion stories of stalking and abuse that're reasons for me to reject these kinds of advertisement.

So I decided to do something. After a few weeks of meeting with the Coca Cola brand team in Addis, the Billboard promoting stalker behaviour has been removed.
They've now replaced it with a different image encouraging persistence. This, obviously, doesn't mean that sexist language won't appear on future advertisements, musics, videos, films... It only means that efforts like this (on and offline) can have some impact. I believe that Coca Cola & all other companies should use their influence & funds to promote respectful behaviour & in this case stand firmly against sexual harassment. I hope it's a good start.
